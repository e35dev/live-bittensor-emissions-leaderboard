# Live Bittensor Emissions Leaderboard

Help Bittensor stakers and subnet builders quickly understand live subnet emissions and recent momentum so they can decide where to stake with confidence.

## Who it serves
Bittensor stakers comparing subnets before allocating stake., Subnet builders monitoring whether their subnet is gaining or losing emissions momentum., Bittensor ecosystem participants who need a fast, trustworthy emissions snapshot without a complex analytics workspace.

## Optimize toward
A restrained, high-performance web leaderboard that feels dense, calm, and trustworthy: live Bittensor subnet emissions are accurate, sortable, timestamped, and paired with clear momentum cues so users can confidently spot staking opportunities at a glance.

## Accept work that
- Open the leaderboard and immediately see all relevant subnets ranked by current emissions, with the most important values visible above the fold on desktop.
- Sort the leaderboard by emissions, subnet identifier/name, recent momentum, and any other displayed numeric column, with the active sort direction clearly visible.
- Scan each subnet row for current emissions, rank, last-known change or momentum, and a compact sparkline that communicates recent direction without requiring a drill-down.
- Compare a few candidate subnets at a glance and identify which ones are gaining, flat, or losing emissions momentum.
- Check the last-updated timestamp to confirm the data is fresh before making a staking decision.
- Understand exactly why a subnet appears where it does: rankings and sorts must be based only on visible column values, with no hidden weighting or opaque scoring.

## Close work that
- Do not create a Bloomberg-terminal style interface with crowded toolbars, dense filters, or expert-only visual noise.
- Do not add heavy configurable analytics, custom weighting models, or opaque ranking scores.
- Do not make users configure a dashboard before they can see emissions data.
- Do not use decorative fluff, oversized hero content, or marketing copy in the core experience.
- Do not expose internal development, repository, review, or maintainer mechanics in customer-facing UI.

## Keep it feeling
- Linear-like calm density: crisp typography, tight spacing, strong alignment, minimal decoration.
- CoinGecko-like table utility: sortable columns, clear numeric hierarchy, compact momentum sparklines.
- Restrained monochrome base with limited status color for positive, negative, stale, or updating states.
- Fast first impression: no splashy marketing, no heavy dashboards, no unnecessary configuration before seeing data.
- Every visible element should have an obvious decision-support function.
